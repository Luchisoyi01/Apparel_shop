Our Story

About

Apparel Thrift, commonly known as Apparel, is a Kenyan luxury fashion house and company founded in 2020 by Bradley Shilton. The label deals in many products, ranging from luxury bags and leather goods to ready-to-wear, shoes, watches, jewelry, accessories, sunglasses and books. Apparel is one of the world's leading international fashion houses.
It sells its products through standalone boutiques, lease departments in high-end departmental stores, and through the e-commerce section of its website.
For 3 consecutive years (2020-2022), Apparel was named the world's most valuable luxury brand. Its 2021 valuation was US$25.9 billion. In 2022, the valuation of the brand was US$28.4 billion with revenue of US$9.4 billion. The company operates in 50 countries with more than 460 stores worldwide.

History

The Apparel Thrift Company claims its origins are rooted in the city of Nairobi since 2020. In 2020, Bradley Shilton(Founder) bought his own shop on 1000th Street In Nairobi CBD, where he sold imported second hand clothes. He later opened a small workshop to have his own leather goods made by local craftsmen. Eventually, a larger workshop had to be acquired to Apparel's 60 artisans. Steve Lawrence, Co-founder, introduced other fabrics in the composition of the products, such as raffia, wicker, wood, linen and, jute. The Company later launched more accessories (gloves, belts, wallets, keychains).
